    Van Lathan – Professional Career

    Van has evolved into a host and media personality. Previously, he launched The Red Pill, a weekly podcast with roughly 50,000 listeners [each episode]. Today, he is a television personality, a film producer, and the author of his book, in which he describes his funny journey from overweight to skinny. Despite this, he is frequently seen delivering critiques and remarks on his path of becoming in shape.

    Van began his career as a tour guide for The TMZ Tour, a chapter of the adventurous television documentary series Extreme, which debuted in 2012. Later, he began hosting TMZ Live, a live version of the TMZ news program. During his tenure with TMZ Live, he established another podcast called Van Lathan’s The Red Pill, which he started on February 20th, 2018, and ran until December 2019.

    In the podcast, he could be heard discussing the various guests who appeared on television, such as actors, actresses, businesspeople, media figures, and others. He used to cover a variety of themes such as news, politics, pop culture, and so on.

    Van Lathan was named the host of The Ringer, a sports and pop-culture podcast network that debuted on April 12, 2020. While he was working on Ringer, he co-hosted two other podcasts with Jemelle Hill: The Wire: Way Down in the Hole and Higher Learning. Lathan also hosted The Wire: Way Down in the Hole, which premiered on April 15, 2020.

    In this podcast, he and Hill discussed every episode of the HBO drama and crime series The Wire and gave critical critiques of the show. The podcast was broadcast every Monday and Wednesday, or twice a week, from December 2020 through 2021.

    Lathan has also contributed to another well-known podcast, Higher Learning, which aired in May 2020. In the podcast, Lathan and co-host Lindsay examined the viral issues of Black culture, sports, and politics, and invited other guests to discuss the debate. The podcast aired twice a week and is still broadcast on television to this day.

    Lathan has also progressed to the role of executive producer of the Oscar-winning short film Two Distant Strangers, which is about the violence against a black man named Carter and racial society.

    Travon Free, an Emmy-winning personality, writer, and producer, wrote the short film. It was a 32-minute short film that was launched on November 20, 2020, and was viewed online on Netflix on April 9, 2021. The short film won an Oscar for Best Live Action Short Film in 2021, as well as an African-American Film Critics Association Award for Best Short Film the same year.
